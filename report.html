Persona Insights

Elderly User

The Elderly User completed all five tasks successfully but encountered consistent usability friction throughout. Their primary struggle was with discoverability — key information was buried in the footer rather than surfaced in the main navigation, and ambiguous navigation labels forced repeated hovering and scanning. Low contrast on CTA buttons, an unlabelled mobile menu icon, and a hidden search bar compounded the difficulty. Average task completion times hovered around 11.4 seconds, consistently the longest among all personas, with notable hesitation patterns (1200–1800ms) before interacting with footer and navigation elements. Despite completing every task, the experience was marked by uncertainty and inefficient navigation paths.

ADHD User

The ADHD User had the most challenging experience, completing only 1 of 5 tasks (20% pass rate) with an average session time of just 1.54 seconds — indicating extremely rapid abandonment when friction appeared. Two tasks were abandoned due to slow page loads (>8 seconds), one due to missing form field labels, and one due to inability to locate the action button. The user was also consistently distracted by ad-like banner elements, clicking them impulsively before finding task-relevant content. The combination of poor page performance, low-contrast CTAs, hidden navigation paths, and attention-competing promotional elements created an almost entirely inaccessible experience for this persona.

Non-Native English

The Non-Native English User completed all five tasks successfully and showed the fewest confusion points of any persona. However, their interaction patterns revealed elevated hesitation times, particularly when hovering over footer elements (2129–2775ms), suggesting they needed extra time to parse labels and determine navigation paths. The average task time of 10.22 seconds was slightly faster than the Elderly User but significantly slower than the ADHD User's attempts. The one noted confusion point — search bar visibility — was shared with other personas. Overall, the site's content was sufficiently understandable for this persona, though the footer-dependent architecture still forced indirect navigation paths.

Friction Heatmap (9 issues)

Overall Assessment

Usability testing across three personas reveals a site with significant structural and visual design issues that disproportionately impact users with attention differences. The most critical problem is an information architecture that buries essential content — recycling guidelines, business programs, drop-off locations — in the footer, forcing all users into inefficient scroll-based navigation rather than providing clear primary navigation paths. Slow page load times (>8 seconds) caused complete task abandonment for the ADHD persona, while low-contrast CTAs, unlabelled mobile menu icons, and ambiguous navigation labels created friction for elderly users. Form accessibility is also compromised by missing field labels and absent inline validation. While the Non-Native English persona navigated relatively successfully, their elevated hesitation times signal that content discoverability could be improved for all users. The site achieves a 73% overall task success rate, but this masks a stark disparity: 100% success for Elderly and Non-Native English users versus only 20% for the ADHD user, indicating that performance and visual clarity issues are excluding a significant user segment.
